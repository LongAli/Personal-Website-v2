Introduction

As the economy is being impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic, many companies show layoff, freeze hiring or even cancel their internships opportunities. Students need an alternative way to build their experience and resume to compete for the limited opportunities during and after this critical period.

Our team has developed an app that aims to empower and support job seekers with a collaborative project space to build their work experience and provide opportunities to directly connect with recruiters.

Research Question

How might we design a platform in order to help college students improve their chances of getting a future job during and after the pandemic?

Secondary Research

To start off, we performed online research to understand the existing resources that faciliate communication and colloboration for remote studying and working. After that, we decided to focus on the job related perspective and observed communities where people share their recent experience:




To understand the circumstance from both perspectives, we also gathered information from the recruiters side. From the article by Joseph Liu for Forbes and the video by Vadim Liberman& Robin Schooloing, we concluded that:

  • Recruiter need to filter out unqualified applications more effectively due to mass application.
  • Applicants need to be proactive and doing things relevant to their line of work.
  • Candidates can expand their skills interdisciplinarily to be more competitive.
  • Making connections with professionals will significantly increase the chance of getting hired.

Competitor Analysis

From the competitor analysis, we realized that there are a lot of job/internship posting& applications platforms; however, there is a limited number of platforms that provides opportunities for users to build hand-on work experience and enrich career related skills.

And for rograms and/or forums that help people gain hand-on experience by working together in groups, they are not really effective at helping people to connect and lack important features that support group-work such as collaborative workspace.

Hence we want to create an app that dedicates to helping users to connect with others to work on hand-on projects.








So, How does Bluum work?

Demo






1. Onboarding & Home


To ensure that first-time usage is as seamless as possible, the splash pages quickly explains the main functions to a new user.

Upon logging in, you can either create a new project or see recommendations for projects that you might be interested in on the home screen.









2. Create project


There are 2 ways to create a new project:
       a. By clicking "create new project", you can customize project name, description, project roles and deadlines.
       b. Use a "recruiter picked" project template that recruiters from top companies have provide on our platform. User can therefore develop projects following these prompts, which simplifies the team matching experience in remote large-scale company-sponsored project building events, such as hackathons and design sprints.

After clicking "Create", it will direct to project page inside Dashboard. On this page, you can add different links and files, such as creating a team slack link and figma link, or adding project code files into your file forlders.













3. Recruit teammate


When needing more help, user can recruit people by adding more roles into the projects through the "Recruit" button in the project main page.
The project will then become recruiting state.




3a. Cancel recruiting


By clicking the three-dot menu in the name card, users can easily track who has applied for the project upon clicking the person icon.
When users decide NOT to recruit people anymore, they can cancel the recruiting by clicking the trash can icon.



Cancel recruiting

3b. Add new members


To add more members, users can select applicants in the section and add them into the team.

Style guide

As you might notice, throughout the app, we use a consistent color code system to help users distinguish different projects.


For pages that are not project management, we aim to create a more relax feeling with a softer and gradient color block.
As for project management, we use more concrete color blocks to help users better focus on the task they need to do


Takeaways

Find root causes

We got feedback on how people think the concepts of the app are very relatable and that they will definitely use the app like this to connect with others and start projects. It makes me realize that it's pivotal to anchor the most urgent problem in the first place for the following design to work well.

For instance, in both survey and interview response, we also got many responses concerning respondants' emotional states and it turned out that many groups had explored the idea of relaxing and alleviating mental stress. But we studied deeper and figured the root causes that people were anxious and unmotivated because of the cancellation of their internships and that they feel unqualified due to lack of connections and experience. With those root causes in mind, we divergently thought of solutions from different stages including improving skills, getting connections, and applying for jobs. Taking advantage of spotting the right problem and ideating various ideas, our project stands out.


Embrace uncertainty and justify changes

As shown in the iterative design process, our design has gone through several versions and sometimes the content/function of the pages has completely changed. As a team, we discussed those uncertainties carefully while keeping the user need in mind. With reasonable justification, we made improvements.
