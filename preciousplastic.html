About


It's a 12 weeks long side project led by Prof. Eric Hekler and Prof. Andrew Krause from UCSD Design Lab. While the engineering team focus on building and improving the injection machine, solving all the technical problems, the design team that I was in aim for a broader vision on how to build a sustainable and healthy recycling and remaking ecosystem here at San Diego that help unstuck people who also share the same goal as Precious Plastic.

What‚Äôs Precious Plastic?

Started in 2013 by Dave Hakkens, Precious Plastic project builds an open plastic recycling community where EVERYONE can join to recycle, remake and reuse plastic. Now, Precious Plastic has 300+ workspaces worldwide. It provides blueprints for machines and molds along with business starterkit.

Within the Precious Plastic Universe, people can decide to join in different roles to contribute based on their time and resources.

Challenges

Going back to the bigger picture, our main question to solve is that:

How might we help people get unstuck on recycling plastic?



From the interviews with existing PP members, we learned that the lack of various resources including financial, human power, technical knowledge, business promotion etc. is what get they stuck.
As the data came from worldwide(including two from California), it‚Äôs safe to assume that people who are interested in recycling plastic here at San Diego are also facing such obstacles.

Meanwhile, we are also working with TECHNICAL constraint of having responsbilitythe injection machine, SAFETY constraint of potential harmful gas as well as FINANCIAL AND HUMANPOWER constraint of building molds.

Therefore, primary target audiences would be mainly early adopters of repurposed plastic idea. More specific, they are college students who are interested in eco-friendly creation process and products(The 44.2%) as well as local crafters who are interested in recycling plastic on their own.

What we are trying to do...

is that we are trying to build an ecosystem of recycling plastic that is sustainable, accessible and affordable for everyone to take part in.
